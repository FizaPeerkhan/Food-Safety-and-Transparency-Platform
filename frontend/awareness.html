Home Adulteration Tests
Simple tests you can do right now — no lab needed
🥛
Milk
Common adulterants: Water, Starch, Detergent, Urea
Water Test
1
Place a drop of milk on a polished slanting surface.
2
Let it slide down on its own without any push.
🔴 Adulterated: Flows immediately, leaves no white trail behind.
🟢 Pure: Flows slowly, leaves a white trail.
Starch Test
1
Boil a small sample, let cool, add a few drops of iodine solution.
🔴 Adulterated: Turns blue or purple (starch present).
🟢 Pure: No colour change.
🍯
Honey
Common adulterants: Sugar syrup, HFCS, Jaggery
Thumb Test
1
Put a small drop of honey on your thumb.
2
Check if it spills or spreads around.
🔴 Adulterated: Spreads or spills easily.
🟢 Pure: Stays intact on the thumb.
Water Test
1
Add one teaspoon of honey to a glass of water.
🔴 Adulterated: Dissolves and mixes immediately.
🟢 Pure: Settles at the bottom as a lump.
🌿
Turmeric Powder
Common adulterants: Metanil Yellow dye, Chalk powder
HCl Test (Metanil Yellow)
1
Dissolve 1 teaspoon in a glass of warm water.
2
Add a few drops of hydrochloric acid (HCl).
🔴 Adulterated: Turns pink or red (Metanil Yellow present — carcinogenic).
🟢 Pure: No pink or red coloration.
Chalk Test
1
Add turmeric to water and then a few drops of HCl.
🔴 Adulterated: Brisk effervescence (chalk/carbonate present).
🟢 Pure: No or very mild effervescence.
🧈
Ghee & Oils
Common adulterants: Vanaspati, Animal fat, Starch
Baudouin Test (Sesame oil)
1
Mix 1 tsp ghee with equal parts of concentrated HCl + a pinch of sugar.
2
Shake vigorously and let it settle.
🔴 Adulterated: Pink/red colour in acid layer (Vanaspati or sesame oil present).
🟢 Pure: No colour change in acid layer.
🍵
Tea Leaves
Common adulterants: Used tea, Artificial colouring, Sawdust
Filter Paper Test
1
Place a small amount of tea on moist filter paper or white cloth.
2
Press down and then lift to check for staining.
🔴 Adulterated: Paper shows artificial colour stains immediately.
🟢 Pure: Light natural tannin stain only, no bright artificial colour.
🌶️
Chilli Powder
Common adulterants: Brick powder, Sudan Red dye
Water Float Test
1
Add a teaspoon of chilli powder to a glass of water.
2
Stir gently and observe after 2 minutes.
🔴 Adulterated: Red/orange streaks of artificial dye visible; brick powder sinks fast.
🟢 Pure: Water turns slightly orange-red; no streaking or fast sediment.

Decoding Food Labels
What every section of a label actually means
📊 Order by weight
Ingredients are listed from most to least by weight. If sugar is #1, there's more sugar than anything else.
🍬 Hidden sugars
Sugar hides under 50+ names: dextrose, maltose, HFCS, sucrose, cane syrup, fruit juice concentrate, and more.
🔢 E-numbers
Codes like E102, E621 are food additives. Not all are harmful, but unfamiliar ones deserve a quick search.
⚠️ Allergen declaration
Allergens like milk, nuts, gluten, soy, eggs must be declared. Look for "Contains:" or bold text in the ingredient list.
💡 The 5-ingredient rule: Products with 5 or fewer recognisable ingredients are almost always less processed and safer.
🍽️ Serving size trap
Always multiply nutrients by the number of servings you actually eat. A "30g serving" chip packet often has 2–3 servings.
🧂 Sodium watch
Aim for under 2,300mg/day. 600mg+ per serving is high. Processed foods are the #1 source of hidden sodium.
🍬 Added vs total sugar
Newer labels separate "added sugars" from total sugar. Added sugars over 10g/serving is a red flag.
🥑 Fat types matter
Trans fats = 0g always. Saturated fat should be minimal. Unsaturated fats (from nuts, olive oil) are healthy.
⚠️ Even "0g trans fat" labels can legally contain up to 0.499g per serving. Check for "partially hydrogenated oil" in the ingredient list.
🟡 Best Before Date
Product is at peak quality before this date. It may still be safe to consume after, but quality (taste, texture) may decline.
🔴 Use By / Expiry Date
Safety date — do not consume after this date. Critical for dairy, meat, baby food, and medicines.
🟢 Manufacturing Date
Useful to calculate freshness when no expiry is listed. For spices and grains, fresher = more potent flavour.
📦 Storage conditions
Dates are only valid if stored correctly. "Keep refrigerated" products stored at room temperature expire much faster.
🚨 Never consume baby food, dairy, or packaged meat past the Use By date — bacteria grow even without visible spoilage.
🌿 "Natural"
Unregulated term in most countries. Can appear on products with artificial colours and preservatives.
🌾 "Organic"
Only trustworthy with a certified organic seal from APEDA, India Organic, or USDA. Without certification, it's marketing.
💧 "Light" or "Lite"
Can mean light in colour, flavour, or texture — not necessarily calories. Always check the nutrition panel.
🚫 "No Added Sugar"
Means no sugar was added during processing, but the product may still be naturally high in sugar (e.g. fruit juices, flavoured milk).
💡 Rule of thumb: The front of a pack is designed by marketing teams. Always flip to the back for the real story — ingredient list and nutrition facts.

Code Name Found In Concern Level

E320 BHA (Butylated Hydroxyanisole) Chips, biscuits, instant noodles ✕ High
